 Minimal Hacker News

I based this app off my desire to limit work interrupting choices (See The Paradox of Choice by Barry Schwartz if interested in learning more). There are so many unique ways to ingest information, so many ways to design elements that will catch attention, that focus and attention to the task at hand are simply too difficult.

That said, this app just shows a curated group of 10 popular Hacker News items over the last 24 hours. It refreshes once an hour, but because the listing itself is for popular items over the last 24, and so it only changes when something major occurs.

It's not a miracle app: it won't save you from going down the rabbit hole, and endlessly reading. But I've found that if the list doesn't change much, my eyes don't register anything new, and I get back to work. If it helps you, let me know by reaching to me below.

Original codebase is from Adam Albrecht's Hacker News extension (Chrome Store). I still have a lot of his code in this app, purposefully using his javascript while reworking the internals to include the Bootstrap framework.
